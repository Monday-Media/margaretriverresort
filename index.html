The Current Situation

There are some real problems here worth talking about.

Before we talk about what we'd recommend, it's worth being straight about what's working against the business right now. The issues below aren't cosmetic — they're costing real money.

Issue 01

Two brands, no clear architecture

The property operates as the "Margaret River Resort" for accommodation and "The River Hotel" for the pub — but the naming is backwards. A resort that functions like a hotel and a hotel that functions like a pub confuses guests before they've even arrived. The brand needs structure.

Issue 02

The domain situation is a real problem

The most important domains — margaretriverresort.com.au and margaretriverresort.com — appear to be unaccounted for. If the business doesn't own them, someone else does. Separately, mrresort.com.au has been built as an affiliate site using the resort's name and real photography to intercept bookings and route them through trip.com for a commission. That's revenue leaking out of the business right now.

Issue 03

Three booking systems on one website

The Stay page links to book-directonline.com and apac.littlehotelier.com simultaneously, depending on which room type a guest clicks. The restaurant uses a completely different platform again (nowbookit.com). No single system is in charge, and guests are being handed off to external platforms with no brand continuity.

Issue 04

The events page is completely empty

The River Hotel has an active and credible live music program — acts like Paul Dempsey, San Cisco, The Beautiful Girls, Ash Grunwald and more. But the Events page on the website shows a heading and nothing else. No calendar, no listings, no ticket links. For a venue that genuinely competes on live music, this is a significant missed opportunity.

Issue 05

The website is old and the photography is poor

The existing site was built in 2017 and the photography throughout under-serves the property. The footer still lists the previous ownership entity (Macthom Pty Ltd) as the licensee. There's even a typo in the browser title of the About page ("Magaret River" instead of "Margaret River"). Small details, but they tell a story about the level of attention the site has received.

Issue 06

Split social media identity

There are two separate Instagram accounts (@therivermargs with 7,709 followers and @margaretriverresort with just 1,007 and 13 posts). There are also two Facebook pages linked from different parts of the site. Neither identity is fully resourced, and there's no clear strategy about which represents what.

The Opportunity

Margaret River Resort has the makings of one of the most compelling hospitality brands in Western Australia.

Think about what actually exists here. An 89-room hotel in one of Australia's most sought-after tourism destinations. A pub and restaurant with an established live music program that has attracted genuinely notable national and international acts. A bottle shop with its own identity. A function space capable of hosting weddings and corporate events. Pool, tennis, villas. And all of it sitting in Margaret River — a region that sells itself.

The raw ingredients are exceptional. The problem is that nobody on the outside would know it. The current digital presence doesn't come close to reflecting what's actually on offer. The naming is confusing, the website is a decade old, the photography undersells every space, and the booking experience is fragmented enough that guests looking to spend money are being lost before they ever pick up the phone.

Done properly, this becomes a property with a clear, confident brand that guests understand immediately. A website that looks as good as the venue deserves, handles every booking natively, and converts visitors into customers without sending them to third-party platforms. A content library — photography and video — that genuinely shows the place at its best and works across the website, OTA listings, social media, and advertising campaigns for years to come.

The live music program alone is a genuine point of difference for a property of this type. A proper events calendar and the visual assets to support it could make The River Inn one of the most recognised music venues in the South West. The weddings and functions side, with the right photography and a polished online presence, becomes a revenue stream that actively sells itself.

This is not a patch-up job. It's a foundation for what this business can genuinely become.

Everything else depends on getting the brand right first. The naming question — Margaret River Resort as the umbrella, The River Inn underneath — needs to be formalised, not assumed. That decision flows into every domain, every piece of signage, every social handle, and the entire website structure. Getting this wrong at the start means redoing it later. The brand guide that comes out of this phase becomes the single source of truth for everything that follows.

The goal here is simple: one website, one dashboard, no third parties in the middle. Every booking — rooms, restaurant, functions, events — handled natively within the Wix ecosystem, with the operator managing everything from a single back end. The site still connects to the major OTAs for distribution, but direct bookings become the default path for guests who are already engaged. The current chaos of three booking engines, a dead events page, and an outdated design gets replaced with something that actually works as hard as the property does.

Photography and video aren't optional extras on a project like this — they're the foundation that everything else is built on. The new website cannot perform visually without high-quality assets, and every OTA listing performs measurably better with professional photography. Beyond the web build, what comes out of the shoot serves the new brand, social media, advertising campaigns, and functions sales materials for years. We're talking about hundreds of professional images across all areas of the property, a hero video for the website, a 90-second resort film, and a bank of 10 to 15 short-form videos ready for social and paid campaigns. A two to three day shoot in Margaret River. Done once, used everywhere.

Return on Investment

The current situation is costing money. Here's how much.

The numbers below are conservative and based on published benchmarks for Margaret River accommodation, standard OTA commission rates, and the modest assumptions we've outlined. The point isn't to over-promise — it's to show that the cost of doing nothing is already significant.

OTA Commission Savings
$63,700

Estimated annual saving by shifting just 20% of OTA-routed bookings to direct. OTAs charge 15–20% commission per booking. With 89 rooms at 70% occupancy and a conservative $160/night average, the commission leakage is significant. We're not removing OTAs — we're making direct the preferred path.

Based on: 89 rooms × 70% occupancy × $160/night avg × 50% via OTA × 17.5% commission × 20% shift to direct

Additional Event Ticket Revenue
$10,000

A live events calendar on the website, properly maintained and discoverable, makes ticket sales easier to drive. An additional 500 tickets sold across the year at an average of $20 is a conservative and easily achievable target for a venue with an established music program.

Based on: 500 additional tickets × $20 average ticket price

Additional Wedding Revenue
$39,000

A properly built functions page with professional photography and a clear enquiry flow positions the venue to capture weddings it's currently missing. The national average for wedding venue hire in Australia is $15,000–$17,000. We've used a conservative $13,000 per booking for a regional venue, with just three additional weddings over the year.

Based on: 3 additional weddings × $13,000 average venue revenue
