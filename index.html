The Current Situation

There are some real problems here worth talking about.

Before we talk about what we'd recommend, it's worth being straight about what's working against the business right now. The issues below aren't cosmetic — they're costing real money.

Issue 01

Two brands, no clear architecture

The property operates as the "Margaret River Resort" for accommodation and "The River Hotel" for the pub, but the naming is backwards. A resort that functions like a hotel and a hotel that functions like a pub confuses guests before they've even arrived. The brand needs structure.

Issue 02

The domain situation is a real problem

The most important domains, margaretriverresort.com.au and margaretriverresort.com, appear to be unaccounted for. If the business doesn't own them, someone else does. Separately, mrresort.com.au has been built as an affiliate site using the resort's name and real photography to intercept bookings and route them through trip.com for a commission. That's revenue leaking out of the business right now.

Issue 03

Split booking systems with no brand continuity

Room bookings run through Little Hotelier, while restaurant reservations use a completely separate platform (nowbookit.com). Guests are handed off to two different external systems with no consistent experience, and there is no central dashboard for the operator to manage everything from. It is fragmented and unnecessary.

Issue 04

The events page is hard to find and poorly indexed

Events exist on the site but are loaded dynamically via JavaScript, which means search engine bots and AI crawlers see a blank page. The URL is also a broken leftover slug (/copy-of-upcoming-events) rather than something clean and discoverable. For a venue with a genuine live music program, that is a real missed opportunity for organic reach.

Issue 05

The website is old and the photography is poor

The existing site was built in 2017 and the photography throughout under-serves the property. The footer still lists the previous ownership entity (Macthom Pty Ltd) as the licensee. There's even a typo in the browser title of the About page ("Magaret River" instead of "Margaret River"). Small details, but they tell a story about the level of attention the site has received.

Issue 06

Split social media identity

There are two separate Instagram accounts (@therivermargs with 7,709 followers and @margaretriverresort with just 1,007 and 13 posts). There are also two Facebook pages linked from different parts of the site. Neither identity is fully resourced, and there's no clear strategy about which represents what.

The Opportunity

Margaret River Resort has the makings of one of the most compelling hospitality brands in Western Australia.

Think about what actually exists here. An 89-room hotel in one of Australia's most sought-after tourism destinations. A pub and restaurant with an established live music program that has attracted genuinely notable national and international acts. A bottle shop with its own identity. A function space capable of hosting weddings and corporate events. Pool, tennis, villas. And all of it sitting in Margaret River, a region that sells itself.

The raw ingredients are exceptional. The problem is that nobody on the outside would know it. The current digital presence doesn't come close to reflecting what's actually on offer. The naming is confusing, the website is a decade old, the photography undersells every space, and the booking experience is fragmented enough that guests looking to spend money are being lost before they ever pick up the phone.

Done properly, this becomes a property with a clear, confident brand that guests understand immediately. A website that looks as good as the venue deserves, handles every booking natively, and converts visitors into customers without sending them to third-party platforms. A content library of photography and video that genuinely shows the place at its best and works across the website, OTA listings, social media, and advertising campaigns for years to come.

The functions space transformation, combined with the right photography and a polished online presence, turns what is currently an underutilised asset into a revenue stream that actively sells itself to weddings, corporate groups, and private events across the South West.

This is not a patch-up job. It's a foundation for what this business can genuinely become.

Everything else depends on getting the brand right first. The naming question, Margaret River Resort as the umbrella and The River Inn underneath, needs to be formalised, not assumed. That decision flows into every domain, every piece of signage, every social handle, and the entire website structure. The brand guide that comes out of this phase becomes the single source of truth for everything that follows.

The goal is simple: one website, one dashboard, no third parties in the middle. Every booking, rooms, restaurant, functions, events, handled natively within the Wix ecosystem, with the operator managing everything from a single back end. The site still connects to the major OTAs for distribution, but direct bookings become the default path for guests who are already engaged. The current split across two booking platforms and an outdated design gets replaced with something that actually works as hard as the property does.

Photography and video aren't optional extras on a project like this, they're the foundation everything else is built on. The new website cannot perform visually without high-quality assets, and every OTA listing performs measurably better with professional photography. Beyond the web build, what comes out of the shoot serves the new brand, social media, advertising campaigns, and functions sales materials for years. Hundreds of professional images across all areas of the property, a hero video, a 90-second resort film, and a bank of 10 to 15 short-form videos. Done once, used everywhere.

Return on Investment

The numbers make a compelling case.

The figures below are based on actual data provided by management, published benchmarks for Margaret River accommodation, and standard OTA commission rates. Each assumption is stated clearly against the relevant figure. The property is currently running at approximately 90% OTA dependency at 15% average commission, with a room rate moving to $189 per night. These are not generic estimates.

OTA Commission Saving
$289,147

OTAs like Expedia and Booking.com currently account for approximately 90% of all bookings at 15% average commission. The goal is to shift 50% of those bookings to direct over the course of the year. A well-built direct booking pathway, professional OTA listing assets, and a brand guests recognise and trust makes this achievable. We are not removing OTAs from the mix, just making direct the preferred path for guests already engaged with the property.

Based on: 89 rooms x 70% occupancy x 365 nights x 90% via OTA x 50% shift to direct x $189/night x 15% avg commission

Direct Booking Uplift
$184,623

A modern, well-structured website with professional photography, a clear booking path, and strong SEO drives a measurable uplift in overall occupancy. A 3% increase across 89 rooms at $189 per night is a realistic target for a property currently operating with a decade-old digital presence and poor imagery across every platform it appears on. As OTA listing quality improves with professional photography, this figure compounds further.

Based on: 89 rooms x 3% occupancy uplift x 365 nights x $189/night average rate
